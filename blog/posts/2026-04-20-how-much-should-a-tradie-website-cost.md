"How much should I spend on a website for my trade?" is the question every tradie asks before they stop procrastinating and actually get one. The answer is genuinely confusing because Australia has four completely different price tiers — and three of them are a waste of money.

Here's the honest breakdown. No upsells. No bias. Just what each tier actually delivers.

## Tier 1 — DIY: $0 to $30/month

Wix, Squarespace, Weebly, Shopify — drag-and-drop builders marketed at small business owners.

**What you pay:** $0 to $30/month subscription, plus about $15-30/year for a domain name.

**What you get:**
- A template-based website you build yourself.
- Basic hosting included.
- A drag-and-drop editor that works 80% of the time and breaks in weird ways the other 20%.

**What you actually pay:** 30-60 hours of your own time, a site that looks like a template (because it is one), no Australian tradie-specific content, no local SEO setup, no licence-display best practice, and usually no ranking on Google because generic templates don't tick the structured-data boxes Google looks for.

**Who it's for:** hobbyists. Not tradies running real businesses.

## Tier 2 — "Local Web Guy" via Facebook: $500 to $2,000

A mate of a mate, or a guy advertising in the local tradie Facebook group, who'll knock up a WordPress site for you.

**What you pay:** $500-$2,000 upfront. Sometimes a token $30/month for hosting. Sometimes nothing after the build.

**What you get:**
- A WordPress site on a random $5/month host.
- Maybe a theme he bought for $60 and customised.
- No documentation.
- No ongoing support beyond "text me if it breaks".

**The problem:** WordPress sites that aren't actively maintained start breaking in 6-12 months. Plugin updates conflict. The theme stops being supported. Hackers exploit out-of-date code. You ring the guy and he's changed numbers. You end up paying another $1,500 to someone else to fix or rebuild it.

About 60% of tradies in this tier end up with a broken or abandoned site within two years. It's the cheapest tier that looks professional — and the most expensive in long-run reality.

## Tier 3 — Agency: $3,500 to $12,000+

A proper design agency. Glass office. "Discovery workshop". Brand strategy deck. Figma mockups. Project manager.

**What you pay:** $3,500-$12,000+ upfront. Often $150-$400/month for "managed hosting and updates". Usually a 12-month minimum contract.

**What you get:**
- A professionally designed site that takes 6-10 weeks.
- A strategy document nobody on the build team will read.
- Stock photos that aren't your actual work.
- Beautiful typography and a hover animation on the nav.

**The honest truth:** for 95% of tradies, you need the same 5 pages as every other tradie. Home, About, Services, Gallery, Contact. Paying an agency $8,000 for those 5 pages is paying for the agency's overheads — office rent, senior designer salary, project management, discovery workshops — not for a better outcome. The Google ranking signal is the same whether your H1 took 4 hours in Figma or 4 minutes in a template.

**Who it's for:** genuine brand-led businesses with 50+ staff and complex service lines. Not one-ute plumbers or two-person painting crews.

## What you should actually look for

Whichever tier you go with, make sure the site has:

1. Your licence number displayed site-wide.
2. Click-to-call on every mobile page.
3. Service-area content for local SEO.
4. Real photos of your actual work.
5. A quote form that SMS's or emails your phone, not a desktop inbox.
6. Fast load times on mobile (under 3 seconds).
7. Structured data (schema) for Google's answer boxes.

If any of those are missing, the site is a brochure, not a lead machine. And a brochure is worth the $0 tier, not the $10,000 tier.
