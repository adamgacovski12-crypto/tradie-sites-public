You've got a Facebook page with 200 followers, a few reviews, and photos from the last three jobs. Surely that's enough to get you on Google? No. It never was, and in 2026 it's actively costing you leads.

Here's exactly why a Facebook page loses every time to a proper website — and what actually ranks when a homeowner in your suburb types "plumber near me" at 10pm on a Tuesday.

## Google doesn't index Facebook the way you think

Facebook is a walled garden. Most of its content is either behind a login, loaded dynamically via JavaScript that Google's crawlers skim over, or locked inside the Facebook app itself. When Google's ranking algorithm looks at a Facebook business page, it sees a thin wrapper: business name, category, some public posts. That's it.

It sees **nothing** about:

- Your specific services ("emergency hot water repairs", "switchboard upgrades", "colorbond roofing")
- The suburbs you cover
- Your licence number
- Your pricing structure
- Customer testimonials laid out in a readable format
- The kind of structured data (FAQ schema, service schema, breadcrumbs) Google uses to understand your business

A proper website dumps all of that on a clean, crawlable HTML page on your own domain. Google loves it. Facebook hides it.

## The "I searched my business name" trap

Every tradie running a Facebook page has done this: googled their own business name, seen the page pop up, and concluded "yep, I'm on Google". Two problems.

**One**, you'll always rank for your exact business name because Google matches it as a specific string. That's not ranking — that's a name-lookup.

**Two**, 95% of your potential new customers don't know your business name. They type things like:

- "plumber parramatta"
- "licensed electrician near me"
- "best concreter western sydney"
- "emergency plumber 24/7"

For those searches, your Facebook page doesn't appear on page 1, page 2, or anywhere a customer will ever scroll to. The tradie with a proper site does.

## Trust signals — the silent killer

Even when a customer does find your Facebook page, it loses them in about 15 seconds. Why?

A Facebook page:

- Shows up with a generic FB header that looks the same as every side-hustle and every hobby page.
- Doesn't display your licence number anywhere visible.
- Pushes competitors' ads directly next to your content ("other Pages you might like").
- Puts a big fat "Message" button above your phone number.
- Shows your last photo, which might be from 8 months ago.

A proper website:

- Shows your branding and your licence number immediately.
- Puts a click-to-call button right under the hero.
- Tells the customer, in 3 seconds, what you do and where you do it.
- Has no competitor ads.
- Lists every suburb you service and links out to job photos.

Customers aren't snobs. They're just doing risk management. A Facebook page feels like a bloke with a ute. A real site feels like a proper business. They call the business.

## What Google actually ranks in 2026

Short version: sites that look like real businesses, have clear local signals, and give Google clean machine-readable content. More specifically:

- **Your own domain** (even a .com.au for $20-30/yr beats a facebook.com/yourname URL every time)
- **Mobile-first design** (Google's indexer is mobile-first; 80% of searches are mobile)
- **Fast load times** (under 2.5 seconds for Largest Contentful Paint on mobile)
- **Locally-relevant content** (suburb pages, service-area maps, postcodes)
- **Structured data** (Service schema, FAQ schema, LocalBusiness schema)
- **A linked Google Business Profile** with consistent NAP (name, address, phone)
- **A physical or service-area presence** that Google can verify against council/ABR records

A Facebook page scores 0/7 on that list. A proper tradie website scores 7/7 out of the box — if it's built right.
