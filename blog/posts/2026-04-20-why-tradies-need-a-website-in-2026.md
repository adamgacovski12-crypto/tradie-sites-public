Ten years ago a tradie could get away with word-of-mouth, a Facebook page, and a number stuck to the back of the ute. In 2026 that same tradie is invisible — and whoever shows up first on Google is booking the job before your phone even rings.

The homeowner experience has changed. The ute and the tools haven't. What's changed is where the job starts: on a phone, in a Google search, at 9pm on a Sunday, while the hot water tank is leaking into the laundry cupboard.

## The 2026 search reality

About 9 in 10 home-service jobs in Australia now start with a search. Not Instagram, not Facebook groups, not the fridge magnet — Google. And Google ranks proper websites. It doesn't rank Facebook pages. It doesn't rank directory listings the same way it used to. It ranks sites with your own domain, a clear licence, a service-area page, real photos, and speedy load times on mobile.

If you're not in the top 3 local results for "plumber in [your suburb]" you've got a 3% chance of being called. If you're on page 2, you may as well not exist.

## What changed in the last 24 months

Three things tipped the field:

**Google Business Profile alone stopped being enough.** Five years ago you could win local jobs with a well-optimised profile and nothing else. Now Google cross-references your profile against a real website, your reviews, your schema markup, and your service-area content. No website means no backup — and no ranking.

**Customers expect to vet you in 60 seconds.** They land on your site, they look for your licence number, your service areas, and three job photos. If they can't find all three on mobile within a single scroll, they back out and click the next result.

**The AI answer boxes rank structured content.** Modern Google and ChatGPT-style answer boxes pull from sites with FAQ schema, service schema, and clean H2 structure. A Facebook page has none of that. A proper website does.

## What a 2026 tradie website actually does

It's not a brochure. It's an asset that works for you 24 hours a day. The non-negotiables:

- **Click-to-call on every page.** Mobile-first. Thumb-reachable.
- **Your licence number in the header and footer.** Instant trust.
- **Real photos of completed jobs.** Not stock. Not generic "tools on a workbench" images.
- **Service area coverage.** Suburbs, postcodes, or a radius map — Google needs to know where you work.
- **A quote form that lands in your phone.** SMS or email to your mobile, not a desktop inbox you check once a week.
- **Fast on mobile.** If it takes more than 3 seconds to load on a 4G connection, you've lost the customer.

You'll notice what isn't on that list: a hero slider animation, a chat bot that asks for their life story, a "meet the team" page with everyone's dog. That's the old-agency playbook. It doesn't convert.

## The hidden cost of going without

Tradies who put off getting a real site usually say the same thing: "I'm flat out, I don't need more work." Fair enough. But three things quietly happen:

1. **Your rates stay stuck.** The jobs you get from mates and repeat customers pay a set rate. The jobs that come through Google are the ones where you set the terms — and charge the premium.
2. **Your apprentice or son-in-law inherits a dud.** When you sell the business, a Facebook page is worth nothing. A website with 5 years of search history and a domain is worth thousands.
3. **Your competitors compound.** Every month they're online and you're not is another month of Google reviews, ranking signals, and repeat customers stacked in their favour. Starting three years late is genuinely painful.
